Outlier Detection & removal


Outliers are data points that are markedly different from the majority of the data, and they can potentially distort the results of statistical analyses.
From the newly generated feature price_per_sqft:
1)We are considering datapoints where a single bedroom area is less than 300 sqft as outlier and removing them
2)Datapoints where the price_per_sqft is very low or very high is also being considered as a outlier.Lower and higher threshold values are defined by (mean - standard deviation) and (mean + standard deviation) for each location.So keeping rows which are well within the threshold values and dropping the remaining data points.
3)Datapoints whose price_per_sqft is less than price_per_sqft of lesser bhk plot in same location is also an outlier.For Example lets say in 'rajaji Nagar' 1 bhk price per sqft is 5000rupees but price of 2bhk house is 4000rupees - which is an outlier in the data, hence comparing the mean,std and removing it
4)Removing datapoints which has more number of bathrooms than bedrooms, if a property has has 2 bhk and it has more than 5 bathroom means we are considering as outlier and removing it,because more no of bathrooms than bedrooms is very rare and not much effective for a regular house
